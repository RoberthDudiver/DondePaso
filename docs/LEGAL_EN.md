# DondePaso - Terms and Privacy Policy

## 1. Nature of the product

DondePaso is a personal exploration app. Its purpose is to visually show how much of a person’s everyday environment they actually move through.

The revealed map is an approximate representation and not an official source of navigation, safety, health, or precise location.

## 2. Use at the user’s own discretion

The user decides whether to enable permissions such as location, activity recognition, or device security.

Without those permissions, some features may be unavailable or operate in a limited way.

DondePaso does not guarantee:

- absolute accuracy of the revealed map
- uninterrupted passive tracking
- compatibility with every sensor or battery-saving layer
- identical availability on every device

## 3. Current privacy policy

At the moment, DondePaso is designed to operate with local storage and local processing.

That means that for the core experience:

- coordinates are not sent to servers
- personal routes are not uploaded
- no individual remote movement history is created
- user information remains on the device

Local data may include:

- discovered areas
- points
- known kilometers
- daily steps
- experiment-related preferences

## 4. Access security

DondePaso may require fingerprint, face unlock, PIN, or device-level credentials in order to open the user’s local map.

This protection relies on the security already configured on the device.

## 5. Local data deletion

The user can erase progress from inside the app.

Doing so removes the local information associated with DondePaso stored on the device, including discovered areas, points, and movement data saved by the app.

## 6. Possible future use of anonymous and aggregated data

Today the app does not need to send personal data for its core experience.

However, a future version could include an optional module for participating in anonymous or aggregated studies related to urban exploration, such as:

- average known kilometers
- approximate percentage of city familiarity
- broad mobility patterns
- anonymous survey results

If such a module is enabled:

- it should not be on by default
- it should require explicit consent before any transmission
- it should clearly explain which data leaves the device
- it should state the statistical or research purpose
- it should allow the user to decline and still use the app

## 7. Anonymous surveys

In the future, the app may offer optional anonymous surveys to better understand exploration habits, perception of surroundings, or activity levels.

Those surveys should explain:

- whether they are optional
- whether responses stay local or are sent
- whether results are used only in aggregated form

## 8. Transparency and updates

If DondePaso starts collecting any data outside the device, even if anonymous or aggregated, these texts, in-app disclosures, and store declarations must be updated before that functionality is enabled.

## 9. Legal notice

This document is a product and transparency baseline, but it is not a substitute for professional legal advice.

Before publishing in app stores or enabling any remote data collection, these texts should be reviewed with legal counsel according to the relevant jurisdiction.
